Ensemble Architecture

How the Forecast Is Produced

The final probability for each outcome is a weighted average of two inputs: a fundamentals-driven economic model and market-implied pricing from CME Fed Funds futures. The market signal receives the dominant weight because, within 30 days of a meeting, futures markets have historically been the most accurate predictor of Fed actions.

Methodology

Four Independent Signals

The fundamental model decomposes the forecasting problem into four orthogonal signals, each capturing a distinct dimension of Federal Reserve decision-making. The signals are combined through weighted averaging before being blended with market data.

WEIGHT: 25%

Historical Patterns

Frequency of Fed actions over the trailing six months. If the Fed has held steady at recent meetings, the model assigns elevated probability to continued holds—capturing the strong institutional inertia in monetary policy.

WEIGHT: 35%

Economic Indicators

Live data from FRED: CPI year-over-year inflation, the unemployment rate, and the effective Fed Funds rate. Inflation above 3% biases hawkish; below 2% biases dovish. Unemployment above 5% increases cut probabilities.

WEIGHT: 20%

Yield Curve

The 2-year vs. 10-year Treasury spread. A deeply inverted curve signals recession fears and raises cut probabilities. A steep curve suggests economic strength and supports a hawkish hold or hike.

WEIGHT: 20%

Fed Momentum

Analyzes the last three FOMC decisions for policy momentum. A cutting cycle (two or more consecutive cuts) tends to continue. A pause pattern strongly predicts another hold at the next meeting.
